Dead Narratives
Narratives that were the future - until they were not. Click any to read the full story.
2021
'The Supercycle': The Theory That Justified Everything
The supercycle thesis was simple and seductive: Bitcoin's four-year boom-bust cycle was over. Institutional money, ETFs, and mainstream adoption had permanently changed the market structure. There would be corrections, sure. But no more 80% drawdowns. No more multi-year bear markets. The only direction was up.
2022
Stock-to-Flow: The $100K Model That Broke
The Stock-to-Flow (S2F) model was created by an anonymous analyst called PlanB. The thesis was elegant: Bitcoin's value could be predicted by its scarcity ratio (existing supply divided by new production). After each halving, production drops, scarcity increases, and price rises.
2022
'Web3 Will Replace the Internet': What Happened
Between 2021 and 2022, venture capitalists poured over $30 billion into "Web3," the vision that blockchain technology would replace centralized internet platforms. Every app would be decentralized. Users would own their data. Middlemen would be eliminated. The internet would be rebuilt from the ground up.
2022
'NFTs Will Tokenize Everything': They Didn't
In 2021, Beeple sold an NFT for $69 million at Christie's. That single auction convinced millions of people that digital art was the future. Profile picture (PFP) projects exploded. Bored Ape Yacht Club floor prices hit 150 ETH ($400K+). CryptoPunks sold for millions. Everyone was a collector.
2022
The Metaverse: $2 Billion Bet on a Ghost Town
In late 2021, Facebook renamed itself Meta and the crypto world went berserk. Decentraland and The Sandbox became the poster children of the "metaverse," virtual worlds where users could buy, sell, and develop digital real estate using crypto tokens.
2022
Play-to-Earn: The Game Nobody Wanted to Play
Axie Infinity turned gaming into a job. In the Philippines, people quit their day jobs to breed, battle, and trade digital creatures called Axies. At its peak, the game generated $364 million in monthly revenue. Scholarships emerged where investors bought Axie teams and hired players to grind.
