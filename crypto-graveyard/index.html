Crypto Graveyard
11.6 million tokens have failed. These are the ones worth remembering.
2018
BitConnect: The $2.6B Ponzi That Became a Meme
BitConnect promised 1% daily returns through a "trading bot" that never existed. New investor money paid old investor returns. The token hit $463. Then the music stopped.
2019
OneCoin: The $4 Billion Crypto That Never Existed
OneCoin was not a cryptocurrency. It was a multi-level marketing scheme wrapped in crypto branding. It had no blockchain. The "coins" were numbers on a SQL database in Bulgaria. Over 3 million people across 175 countries lost a combined $4 billion.
2021
Squid Game Token: The Rug Pull You Couldn't Escape
In October 2021, a token called SQUID appeared on Binance Smart Chain, riding the hype of the Netflix show Squid Game. It had no connection to the show. No license. No permission. Just a name and a Telegram group.
2022
LUNA Classic: From $40 Billion to a Fraction of a Cent
LUNA was supposed to be the future of money. Its algorithmic stablecoin UST held a $1 peg through a mint-and-burn mechanism with LUNA. When it worked, it looked like magic. When it failed, it was the fastest destruction of wealth in crypto history.
2022
FTT: The Token That Brought Down a $32 Billion Empire
FTT was the native token of FTX, Sam Bankman-Fried's crypto exchange. It was used for fee discounts, staking rewards, and buybacks. On paper, it was a utility token. In reality, it was the collateral underpinning an $8 billion fraud.
2022
Celsius Token: 'Unbank Yourself' - Then Lose Everything
Celsius Network was crypto's version of a high-yield savings account. Deposit your Bitcoin or stablecoins, earn up to 18% APY. CEO Alex Mashinsky appeared on YouTube weekly, assuring depositors their funds were safe. "Banks are not your friends," he said. He was right. He was not their friend either.
2023
SafeMoon: The 'Safe' Token That Wasn't
SafeMoon launched in March 2021 with a novel hook: a 10% tax on every sale. Half went to existing holders as "reflections." Half was burned. The pitch was that selling was expensive, so everyone would hold, and the price would only go up.
