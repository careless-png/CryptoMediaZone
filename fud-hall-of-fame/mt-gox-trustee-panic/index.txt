3:T4a0,Mt. Gox collapsed in 2014 after losing 850,000 BTC. The bankruptcy trustee recovered roughly 142,000 BTC  -  worth hundreds of millions initially, and eventually tens of billions.

For a decade, the Mt. Gox trustee wallet became the most watched address in Bitcoin. Every time on-chain watchers detected movement, panic selling ensued. "Mt. Gox is dumping!" became a reflexive fear trigger regardless of whether actual selling was happening.

In 2018, trustee Nobuaki Kobayashi had been selling BTC on open markets rather than distributing directly. Analysis suggested his sales contributed to Bitcoin's decline from $17,000 to $6,000. He reportedly sold over 35,000 BTC worth nearly $400 million at depressed prices.

After community backlash, the trustee stopped market-selling. But for years afterward, any movement from associated wallets triggered sell-offs, even internal transfers. Bots and Twitter accounts amplified every transaction into a market event.
