The Rap Sheet
Crypto's most notorious criminals. Fraudsters, hackers, and fugitives. Every charge documented.
2013
Ross Ulbricht: The Dread Pirate Roberts
Ross Ulbricht was a 26-year-old physics grad from Austin, Texas who decided the best use of his talents was building the Amazon of illegal drugs. In 2011, he launched Silk Road - a Tor-hidden marketplace where you could buy anything from weed to fake IDs using Bitcoin. He operated under the alias "Dread Pirate Roberts," a reference to The Princess Bride that became the most infamous pseudonym in crypto history.
2014
Mark Karpelès: The Man Who Lost 850,000 Bitcoin
Mark Karpelès was a French developer living in Tokyo who somehow ended up running the world's largest Bitcoin exchange. He'd acquired Mt. Gox in 2011 from its creator Jed McCaleb (who would later co-found Stellar and Ripple). At its peak, Mt. Gox handled over 70% of all Bitcoin transactions on Earth.
2017
Ruja Ignatova: The Cryptoqueen
Dr. Ruja Ignatova is the most successful crypto scammer who ever lived - and she's still free. The Bulgarian-German businesswoman created OneCoin in 2014, pitching it as the "Bitcoin killer" to audiences of thousands at stadium-sized events. She wore designer gowns, quoted cryptography papers, and promised investors they were buying the future.
2017
Alexander Vinnik: The BTC-e Laundromat
Alexander Vinnik was a Russian national who operated BTC-e, one of the earliest and shadiest cryptocurrency exchanges. From 2011 to 2017, BTC-e processed an estimated $4 billion in transactions with minimal-to-zero know-your-customer requirements. It was the go-to exchange for ransomware operators, darknet markets, and anyone who needed to clean dirty Bitcoin.
2017
Zhimin Qian: The Goddess of Wealth
Zhimin Qian - aka "Yadi Zhang," aka "The Goddess of Wealth" - orchestrated a fraud so audacious it reads like rejected fiction. Between 2014 and 2017, she ran a massive investment pyramid through her company Lantian Gerui, defrauding over 128,000 Chinese citizens - many of them taxi drivers, butchers, and farmers who invested their life savings. Total take: approximately $6.6 billion.
2017
Lazarus Group: North Korea's Crypto Army
The Lazarus Group is not a person - it's a cyber army. Operated by North Korea's Reconnaissance General Bureau (their intelligence agency), Lazarus has become the most prolific and successful crypto theft operation in history. They don't hack for profit. They hack to fund a nuclear weapons program.
2018
Satish Kumbhani: BitConnect's Phantom
"Hey hey heyyyy! Wassa wassa wassa BITCONNEEEECT!" Carlos Matos screaming at a BitConnect conference became one of crypto's most iconic memes. But behind the meme was a very real $2.4 billion Ponzi scheme, and behind the Ponzi was Satish Kumbhani - an Indian national who quietly ran the operation while the promoters soaked up attention.
2021
Fatih Özer: The Man Who Robbed Turkey and Ran
Fatih Faruk Özer was the 28-year-old CEO of Thodex, Turkey's largest cryptocurrency exchange. In April 2021, with Turkey's economy in crisis and the lira in freefall, millions of Turks had turned to crypto as a hedge. Thodex was their platform of choice.
2022
Lichtenstein & Razzlekhan: The Bitfinex Bonnie & Clyde
In August 2016, hackers breached Bitfinex and stole 120,000 Bitcoin - worth about $72 million at the time. The trail went cold. For six years, blockchain investigators tracked the stolen funds as they slowly moved through thousands of wallets, mixing services, and chain-hopping maneuvers.
2022
Sam Bankman-Fried: The $8 Billion Fraud
Sam Bankman-Fried - SBF - was crypto's most successful con man because he didn't look like one. The MIT physics grad with the messy hair, cargo shorts, and beanbag-chair office convinced the world he was a genius altruist who just happened to run a crypto exchange. He was on magazine covers. He met with regulators. He donated millions to politicians. He testified before Congress about the need for responsible crypto regulation.
2022
Su Zhu & Kyle Davies: Three Arrows Capital
Su Zhu and Kyle Davies were the founders of Three Arrows Capital (3AC), a crypto hedge fund that became legendary for two things: its size and its founders' unshakeable arrogance. Su Zhu in particular became notorious for his Twitter proclamations about "supercycles" and his absolute conviction that crypto could only go up.
2022
Do Kwon: The $40 Billion Vaporizer
Do Kwon was the most arrogant man in crypto, and that's a competitive field. The Stanford-educated South Korean founder of Terraform Labs created LUNA and its algorithmic stablecoin UST, which maintained its $1 peg through a mechanism involving burning LUNA tokens. He was openly contemptuous of critics, famously tweeting "I don't debate the poor" to anyone who questioned the model.
2022
Alex Mashinsky: Celsius's Lying Machine
Alex Mashinsky was a serial entrepreneur who co-invented VOIP technology (voice over internet protocol) and then decided to apply his talents to financial fraud. In 2017, he founded Celsius Network with the catchphrase "unbank yourself" - a crypto lending platform that offered depositors yields of up to 18% on their crypto.
2023
CZ Zhao: Binance's $4.3 Billion Fine
Changpeng Zhao - CZ - built Binance into the largest cryptocurrency exchange on Earth. At its peak, Binance handled more trading volume than all its competitors combined. CZ became one of the richest people in the world, with a net worth estimated at $65 billion. He did this by deliberately ignoring anti-money laundering laws.
2023
Justin Sun: The Teflon Troll of Crypto
Justin Sun is the closest thing crypto has to a cockroach - and he'd probably take that as a compliment. The TRON founder, BitTorrent acquirer, and Grenadian diplomat has been accused of fraud, market manipulation, and wash trading by the SEC. He's been investigated by multiple countries. And yet he continues to operate, tweet, and provoke as though nothing has happened.
2024
Roger Ver: Bitcoin Jesus Falls From Grace
Roger Ver was crypto's first celebrity. In the early 2010s, he was the loudest, most passionate Bitcoin evangelist alive - earning the nickname "Bitcoin Jesus." He invested in some of crypto's earliest companies: Blockchain.com, Kraken, BitPay, Ripple. He reportedly held around 400,000 BTC at one point. He was a true believer before anyone else.
