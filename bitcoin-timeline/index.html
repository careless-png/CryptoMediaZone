Bitcoin Timeline
The moments that shaped crypto history. Click any to read the full story.
2009
The Genesis Block: January 3, 2009 - It All Starts Here
On January 3, 2009, Satoshi Nakamoto mined the first Bitcoin block. Embedded in the coinbase transaction was a message: "The Times 03/Jan/2009 Chancellor on brink of second bailout for banks."
2010
Bitcoin Pizza Day: 10,000 BTC for Two Pizzas
On May 22, 2010, programmer Laszlo Hanyecz posted on the Bitcoin forum: "I'll pay 10,000 bitcoins for a couple of pizzas." Someone took him up on it and ordered two Papa John's pizzas delivered to Hanyecz's house in Jacksonville, Florida.
2011
Silk Road: Bitcoin's First Killer App Was Illegal
Silk Road was an anonymous marketplace on the Tor network where users could buy drugs, fake IDs, and other contraband using Bitcoin. Founded by Ross Ulbricht (alias "Dread Pirate Roberts") in 2011, it became Bitcoin's first major use case.
2012
The First Halving: When Bitcoin Learned Scarcity
On November 28, 2012, Bitcoin's block reward dropped from 50 BTC to 25 BTC. It was the first "halving," a pre-programmed event that occurs every 210,000 blocks (roughly every four years). It was the moment Bitcoin's deflationary monetary policy went from theory to practice.
2020
Stablecoins: The $150 Billion Bridge Between Two Worlds
Stablecoins are the most practically useful innovation in cryptocurrency, and they get almost no hype. Tokens pegged to the US dollar, like USDT (Tether), USDC (Circle), and DAI (MakerDAO), collectively exceed $150 billion in circulation and regularly process more daily volume than Bitcoin.
2024
The Bitcoin ETF: Wall Street Finally Says Yes
On January 10, 2024, the SEC approved 11 spot Bitcoin ETFs simultaneously. It was the most anticipated regulatory decision in crypto history. The Winklevoss twins had first applied for a Bitcoin ETF in 2013. It took over a decade of rejections, lawsuits, and lobbying to get to yes.
2024
Bitcoin Hits $100K: The Number Everyone Said Was Impossible
In late 2024, Bitcoin crossed $100,000 for the first time. The milestone that had been a meme since 2017, a laser-eye prophecy in 2021, and a shattered dream in 2022, finally arrived.
2024
Trump Embraces Crypto: From 'Scam' to Strategic Reserve
In 2019, President Trump tweeted: "I am not a fan of Bitcoin and other Cryptocurrencies, which are not money, and whose value is highly volatile and based on thin air." By 2024, he was headlining Bitcoin conferences and promising to make America "the crypto capital of the world."
