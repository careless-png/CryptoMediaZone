3:T4e6,In late 2024, Bitcoin crossed $100,000 for the first time. The milestone that had been a meme since 2017, a laser-eye prophecy in 2021, and a shattered dream in 2022, finally arrived.

The path to six figures was anything but straight. Bitcoin had to survive Mt. Gox, China bans, the ICO crash, COVID Black Thursday, the LUNA collapse, the FTX fraud, and years of obituaries declaring it dead. Each crisis was supposed to be the end. None of them were.

The catalyst for the final push was the ETF approval in January 2024, which opened the floodgates to institutional money. The April 2024 halving reduced supply growth. A pro-crypto political shift in the US added regulatory tailwinds. The confluence of factors pushed BTC through the psychological barrier.

$100,000 Bitcoin meant different things to different people. For early adopters, it was validation. For institutions, it was a portfolio allocation. For skeptics, it was cognitive dissonance. For the millions who bought at $60,000 in 2021 and held through the $15,000 bottom, it was relief.
