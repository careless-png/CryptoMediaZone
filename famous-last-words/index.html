Famous Last Words
The worst predictions ever made about crypto. All verified. All spectacularly wrong.
2021
Laser Eyes: '$100K by End of 2021'
In February 2021, a movement swept crypto Twitter. Change your profile picture to have laser eyes. Signal to the world that Bitcoin is going to $100,000 by the end of the year. Politicians, CEOs, influencers, and random anons all joined in. The laser eyes became a tribal badge of conviction.
2022
Do Kwon: 'I Don't Debate the Poor'
Do Kwon was the king of crypto Twitter arrogance. When critics questioned whether UST's algorithmic peg could hold, he responded with "I don't debate the poor." When analysts posted threads explaining the death spiral risk, he tweeted "your size is not size." When a reporter asked about risk management, he said LUNA was "mathematically impossible to fail."
2022
SBF: 'FTX Is Fine. Assets Are Fine.' - 4 Days Before Collapse
On November 7, 2022, as rumors swirled about FTX's solvency, Sam Bankman-Fried tweeted: "FTX is fine. Assets are fine." He followed up with: "FTX has enough to cover all client holdings. We don't invest client assets."
2022
Mashinsky: 'Your Funds Are Safe'
Every Friday, Alex Mashinsky appeared on Celsius Network's weekly AMA wearing his trademark Celsius t-shirt. Every Friday, someone asked if funds were safe. Every Friday, he said yes. "Your deposits are safe." "We're in a strong position." "Celsius is built different."
2023
BitBoy: 'This Coin Will 100x'
Ben Armstrong, better known as BitBoy Crypto, was the most followed crypto YouTuber in the world. At his peak, he had 1.4 million subscribers and massive influence over retail trading decisions. When he said "this coin will 100x," thousands of people bought.
