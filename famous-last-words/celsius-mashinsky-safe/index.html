Mashinsky: 'Your Funds Are Safe'

He said funds were safe on live streams. He was withdrawing his own millions at the same time.

Every Friday, Alex Mashinsky appeared on Celsius Network's weekly AMA wearing his trademark Celsius t-shirt. Every Friday, someone asked if funds were safe. Every Friday, he said yes. "Your deposits are safe." "We're in a strong position." "Celsius is built different."

While he was saying these words on camera, he was privately selling $68 million worth of CEL tokens. His own money was heading for the exits while he was telling 1.7 million users to stay.

The contrast between Mashinsky's public reassurances and his private actions is what elevated this from business failure to criminal fraud. He didn't just make bad investments with customer money. He actively lied to customers while extracting his own capital.

Court documents revealed that Mashinsky was aware of Celsius's deteriorating financial position months before the withdrawal freeze. Internal communications showed executives discussing the growing shortfall. Yet the weekly AMAs continued, the reassurances continued, and the "Unbank Yourself" marketing continued.

On June 12, 2022, Celsius froze all withdrawals. The Friday AMAs stopped. Mashinsky eventually resigned and was arrested in July 2023. He pleaded guilty to fraud charges and was sentenced to 12 years in prison.

"Your funds are safe" is now the crypto version of "the checks are in the mail." Whenever a protocol pauses withdrawals and the CEO starts doing emergency AMAs, the community knows exactly what comes next.

The Aftermath

'Your funds are safe' joins 'assets are fine' as crypto's most toxic reassurance. The phrase is now treated as an immediate red flag by experienced investors.
