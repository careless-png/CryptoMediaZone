Exchange Graveyard
Collapsed exchanges. $15B+ in user funds gone. Click any to read the full story.
2014
Mt. Gox: The Exchange That Lost Everything
Mt. Gox started as a Magic: The Gathering card trading site. Its name literally stands for "Magic: The Gathering Online eXchange." Mark Karpeles repurposed it for Bitcoin in 2010, and by 2013 it handled 70% of all Bitcoin transactions on Earth.
2017
BTC-e: The Exchange That Laundered $4 Billion
BTC-e was one of the oldest and largest Bitcoin exchanges, operating from 2011 to 2017. It was known for lax KYC requirements and became the exchange of choice for anyone who did not want to be identified. That included money launderers, ransomware operators, and whoever stole the Mt. Gox Bitcoin.
2019
QuadrigaCX: The Dead CEO's Missing $190 Million
Gerald Cotten was the 30-year-old CEO of QuadrigaCX, Canada's largest cryptocurrency exchange. In December 2018, he reportedly died of Crohn's disease complications while on his honeymoon in India. His wife reported his death and provided a death certificate.
2019
Cryptopia: Hacked, Liquidated, Forgotten
Cryptopia was a New Zealand-based exchange popular with altcoin traders. It listed hundreds of small-cap tokens that larger exchanges ignored. In January 2019, it was hacked for approximately $16 million. The exchange attempted to reopen but went into liquidation in May 2019.
2022
FTX: From $32 Billion to Bankruptcy in 10 Days
FTX was the golden child of crypto. Sam Bankman-Fried built it into a $32 billion empire with celebrity endorsements, a Super Bowl ad featuring Larry David, a stadium naming deal, and political donations that made him the second-largest donor to the Democratic Party.
2022
Celsius: 'Unbank Yourself' Into Bankruptcy
Celsius Network was a crypto lending platform that promised high yields on deposits. "Unbank yourself" was the slogan. CEO Alex Mashinsky became a YouTube personality, hosting weekly AMAs where he assured depositors their funds were safe. At its peak, Celsius held $20 billion in assets.
2022
Voyager Digital: The Broker That Bet It All on 3AC
Voyager Digital was a publicly traded crypto brokerage that positioned itself as the mainstream-friendly option. Listed on the Toronto Stock Exchange, regulated, with a clean app interface. CEO Stephen Ehrlich appeared on CNBC and Bloomberg regularly.
