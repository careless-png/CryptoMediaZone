The Human Cost
Real lives destroyed by crypto. Not statistics. People. Told with the respect they deserve.
2014
Autumn Radtke: The CEO Who Couldn't Take Any More
Autumn Radtke was the 28-year-old CEO of First Meta, a Singapore-based digital assets exchange. In February 2014, she was found dead outside her apartment building. Her death was ruled a suicide.
2018
Matthew Mellon: $1 Billion in XRP, Dead at 54
Matthew Mellon was an heir to two of America's most prominent banking families - the Mellons and the Drexels. In 2017, he invested heavily in XRP (Ripple) when it was trading for fractions of a cent. By early 2018, his holdings were reportedly worth over $1 billion.
2018
Gerald Cotten: The Dead Man's Switch That Locked $190 Million
Gerald Cotten was the 30-year-old CEO of QuadrigaCX, Canada's largest cryptocurrency exchange. In December 2018, while on honeymoon in India, he reportedly died of complications from Crohn's disease. He was the sole person with access to the exchange's cold wallets containing approximately $190 million in customer cryptocurrency.
2021
Mircea Popescu: The Bitcoin Billionaire Who Drowned
Mircea Popescu was one of Bitcoin's earliest and most controversial large holders. A Romanian programmer and blogger, he had accumulated an estimated 50,000+ BTC over the years, making him one of the wealthiest individuals in cryptocurrency.
2022
Nikolai Mushegian: The Tweet Before the Drowning
Nikolai Mushegian was a 29-year-old computer scientist and co-founder of MakerDAO, one of the most important protocols in decentralized finance. On October 28, 2022, he posted a tweet that would become one of the most haunting messages in crypto history. He claimed that the CIA, Mossad, and the "pedo elite" were going to murder him and make it look like an accident.
2022
Tiantian 'TT' Kullander: Gone at 30
Tiantian Kullander, known as TT, was the co-founder of Amber Group, a Hong Kong-based crypto finance firm valued at $3 billion. On November 23, 2022, he died "unexpectedly in his sleep." He was 30 years old.
2022
Javier Biosca: Spain's Crypto Ponzi King
Javier Biosca was a Spanish crypto broker and founder of Algorithms Group, accused of running a Ponzi scheme worth over €500 million. In November 2022, he fell from the fifth floor of a hotel in Estepona, Spain. He was dead on impact.
2022
Vyacheslav Taran: Helicopter Crash Over Monaco
Vyacheslav Taran was a 53-year-old Russian crypto entrepreneur and co-founder of Libertex, one of Europe's largest online trading platforms. On November 25, 2022, his helicopter crashed in the mountains near Villefranche-sur-Mer, between Nice and Monaco in the south of France. He was killed along with the pilot.
2022
The LUNA Victims: Lives Destroyed by a Death Spiral
When the LUNA/UST ecosystem collapsed in May 2022, it wiped out approximately $40 billion in value in less than a week. The algorithmic stablecoin that was supposed to be safe turned out to be a mathematical death spiral. The financial losses were catastrophic. The human losses were worse.
2022
Park Mo: The LUNA Executive Found Dead
In December 2022, Park Mo, a South Korean cryptocurrency executive with ties to the Bithumb exchange, was found dead outside his residence. The circumstances pointed to suicide, though full details were not made public by Korean authorities.
2023
Fernando Pérez Algaba: Dismembered Over Crypto Debts
Fernando Pérez Algaba was a 41-year-old Argentine businessman who had become a social media personality flaunting a lavish lifestyle reportedly funded by cryptocurrency investments. In July 2023, his dismembered body was found in a suitcase near a stream in the suburbs of Buenos Aires. Children discovered the remains.
2025
Fatih Özer: 11,196 Years in Prison, Then Suicide
Fatih Özer was the founder and CEO of Thodex, Turkey's largest cryptocurrency exchange. In April 2021, he shut down the platform and fled the country with an estimated $2 billion in customer funds. Over 400,000 Turkish investors lost their savings overnight.
2025
David Balland: The Ledger Kidnapping That Shook France
In January 2025, David Balland, co-founder of Ledger - the hardware wallet company that protects billions of dollars in cryptocurrency - was kidnapped along with his wife from their home in France. The attackers severed one of Balland's fingers and sent video of the mutilation to Ledger executives, demanding €10 million in ransom.
2025
Konstantin 'Kostya Kudo' Galich: Assassinated in Broad Daylight
Konstantin Galich, known online as Kostya Kudo, was a 32-year-old Ukrainian cryptocurrency trader who managed approximately $65 million in client funds, reportedly including money from Ukrainian military intelligence officials. On October 11, 2025 - one day after the devastating crypto market crash - he was found dead in his Lamborghini Urus in Kyiv with a gunshot wound to the head.
2025
Alexei Dolgikh: The Crypto Millionaire Who Died at 200mph
In November 2025, Alexei Dolgikh, a Russian cryptocurrency millionaire, was killed when his Lamborghini Urus crashed at high speed in Moscow. The vehicle struck a barrier, overturned, and caught fire. Two of the four passengers died, including Dolgikh.
2025
Roman & Anna Novak: Kidnapped, Tortured, Left for Dead
Roman and Anna Novak were a Russian couple living in Dubai. Roman had a complicated past - he was a convicted crypto fraudster who had run Fintopio, a platform accused of raising over $500 million through questionable means. Despite his criminal history, the couple was living openly in the UAE with their young children.
2025
The Wrench Attack Epidemic: When They Come for Your Keys
In 2025, the phenomenon known as "wrench attacks" - a reference to the xkcd comic about bypassing encryption by hitting someone with a wrench - became a full-blown epidemic. At least 72 verified physical attacks against cryptocurrency holders were recorded, a 75% increase from 2024. The total stolen through violence reached $40.9 million.
