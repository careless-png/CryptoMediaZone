The Binance Bank Run That Wasn't: How a Tweet Faked a $17B Exodus

2026-02-14

It started, as these things always do, with a screenshot. On February 3, 2026, Binance's withdrawal system went dark for exactly twenty minutes. Twenty. Minutes. From 02:23 to 02:43 GMT, you couldn't pull your coins off the world's largest exchange. Trading kept running. The order books didn't skip a beat. But for twenty minutes, the withdrawal button didn't work. In any other industry, a twenty-minute maintenance window wouldn't make the news. In crypto, it's a five-alarm fire. Within hours, the screenshots started circulating. The threads started building. And Crypto Twitter did what Crypto Twitter does best: it panicked. By February 11, a viral tweet claimed $17 billion had left Binance in seven days. Over 100,000 views. Comparisons to FTX. 'This is how it starts,' people said. There was just one problem: the number was wrong. Not a little wrong. Fifty times wrong.
